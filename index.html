Help & FAQ
How does the meal suggestion work?

CookCompass picks a dish you haven't cooked recently, and pairs it with a suitable side. If nutrient-smart picks is on, it leans toward dishes that cover your weekly gaps in protein, iron, calcium, or fiber.

Why does it keep suggesting the same dishes?

It avoids repeating a dish within 7 days. If you have a small cooking history, the pool of "recent" dishes shrinks and suggestions may feel repetitive. Mark more meals as cooked to help it learn your pattern.

What does "Mark as Cooked" do?

It logs the meal into your history. This drives two things — the nutrient tracker updates with what you actually ate, and the suggestion engine avoids repeating that dish for the next 7 days.

Is my data saved anywhere?

All your history and settings are saved on your device only — nothing is sent to any server. The data lives inside your browser, so it will be lost if you: clear your browser/site data, use incognito or private mode, switch to a different browser, or get a new phone. This is exactly why the Export backup option exists — it lets you download your data as a file so you can restore it anytime, or move it to another device.

How do I back up or move my data?

Go to ⚙️ Settings → Export backup. This downloads a JSON file. To restore, use Import backup on any device with the same file.

What are nutrient-smart picks?

When enabled, the app checks which nutrients (protein, calcium, iron, fiber) you've had less of this week and prioritises dishes that cover those gaps. You can toggle this off in ⚙️ Settings for pure variety-based suggestions.

Does this work offline?

Yes — once the page is loaded, everything works without an internet connection. Your data is local and the app needs no server to function.

The nutrition numbers — how accurate are they?

They are approximate values for the main dish only, based on typical home-cooked portions with moderate oil. Side dishes are not included. Use them as a relative guide, not precise medical data.

How does the Grocery list work?

The Grocery tab automatically builds a list of ingredients from your meals cooked in the past 7 days, plus today's suggested meals. The "Everyday Ingredients" section below it shows common South Indian pantry staples. Use the search bar to quickly find a specific item, and tick items off as you shop.
